Bentota Turtle Hatchery

Bentota- the stetch of Golden Sand in Sri Lanka

Bentota is a small beach resort town in the southern region of Sri Lanka, some 62 kilometres from Colombo, the country's capital. Bentota is well known for its popular beach, which both locals and visitors adore. The village is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and verdant surroundings, with towering palm trees predominating. North of the village is the Bentota River, which is incredibly clear. It is bordered by Bentota National Holiday Resort Complex, the most well-known resort in Sri Lanka. The most opulent and popular vacation spot in the nation is this resort. A variety of water sports are available at Bentota Beach, including as scuba diving, snorkelling, and watching turtles.

Turtle Hatcheries of Bentota

Sea turtles who come to our idyllic island to lay their eggs as well as ill, damaged, and injured turtles are all protected and cared for by Bentota turtle hatcheries. The best thing is that by assisting at the hatchery, you can see these initiatives in action and get firsthand knowledge. Here is a must if you're looking for activities to do in Bentota!. Numerous initiatives undertaken by them are targeted at improving turtle welfare and raising public awareness of this critically endangered animals. Several of their projects are:

Night Patrols

Who claimed that the beach wasn't attractive at night? Patrolling the beach at night is one of the hatcheries' programmes, which not only serves to stop poachers but also allows visitors to see lovely female turtles approaching their nests. A once-in-a-lifetime opportunity that shouldn't be passed up.

Education & Awareness

It is actively committed in raising awareness of the value of protecting these endangered species among visitors and the local population. They accomplish this through neighbourhood events and awareness initiatives, which allow for the collection of a plethora of data.

Hatchery

Collecting eggs from nests on the shore and reburying them within the hatchery, where they may safely hatch, makes up a significant portion of the hatchery's job. These hatchlings are later returned to their natural habitat.

Overview

Sri Lanka places a lot of emphasis on turtle conservation and works hard to save these lovely animals. Turtles can be protected, treated, and released back into the wild with the help of turtle hatcheries located all around the nation. Typically, turtle eggs are removed from the shore to reduce the chance of theft by poachers. After hatching, the young turtles are watched over until they are old enough to be released.
